WHAT IS SCIENTAIL?

Simply put, Scientail is a nonprofit organization dedicated to providing the youth of the Cary-Morrisville with hands-on learning experiences in the field of Science, Technology, Engineering, Math, and Art! We do this through a combination of in person and online events, to encourage participation in our mission of getting kids the skills they need to pursue stem careers in later life.





  • WHY SCIENTAIL?

    Why not! Scientail is the best option for hands-on activities related to STEM for completely NO PRICE for kids that need it. We provide a level of hands-on that is not seen anywhere else.

    EXPLORE

  • WHO IS SCIENTAIL?

    We are a group of 6 high schoolers, creating dreams for many. We hope to help the STEM field grow. This same group of 6 high school students coded this website from scratch and make up the Board of Directors!

    OUR TEAM

  • WHERE IS SCIENTAIL?

    In-person activities will be organized in picnic shelters in various parks around Cary. Most events will be 1-2 hours long, and centered around either engineering, science, art, math, or technology. Keeping in mind the current situation, class sizes will be small and masking mandatory.

HANDS-ON ACTIVITIES

Hands-On Activities to help kids strive in STEM!


In-person activities will be organized in picnic shelters in various parks around Cary. Most events will be 1-2 hours long, and centered around either engineering, science, art, math, or technology. Utilizing short lessons, crafting activities, and real-world examples, we aim to pique the youths interests and show them whats possible with a stem career. Keeping in mind the current situation, class sizes will be small and masking mandatory. Click here for an event calendar.



Solar System Scaling!

Students will make a scaled model of the Solar System using string and beads! These students will learn ratios, basic distance calculations, how vast the Solar System really is, and more!


Ice Cream Crazy!

Students will make ice cream in a bag using heavy whipping cream, sugar, vanilla, ice, and salt! These students will learn about physical and chemical reactions and how liquid turns to solid!


Newton's Rocket

Students will make a double stage or one stage rocket that will fly across the room using balloons and straws! These students will learn about Newton's third law, speed / velocity, and aerodynamics!





HOW CAN YOU HELP OUT?

As with most nonprofits, donations are our main source of funding. All funds go to ensuring the future of Scientail as a nonprofit built on helping elementary schoolers get hands-on experience with the STEM field. You can also volunteer to help out with certain activities throughout the year. Scientail also plans to have locations to where you can donate used or old computers or other devices.
